## Design Context

### Design Mode
Brand-creative — UofG design system (`uofg-design-system` skill) provides the foundation.

**Locked:** UofG colour palette (university blue #011451, dark blue #005398, greys, error/success/highlight), Noto Sans as primary typeface, 4px spacing scale, WCAG AA contrast on text.

**Creative latitude:** Layout composition, secondary display font, motion, spatial rhythm, surface treatments, severity colour derivations beyond the two existing status colours.

### Users
IT management at a UK university (University of Glasgow). These are heads of infrastructure teams, service managers — people who oversee sysadmins but don't SSH into servers themselves. They review reports in a browser, possibly on a laptop in an office or a meeting room. They want to know: are we in good shape, and if not, where should I direct my team's attention? They may share the report upward to directors or across to other service owners.

### Brand Personality
Steady, watchful, competent.

This is an internal operations tool, not a public-facing university page. It should feel like a well-run operations centre — confident when things are green, clearly urgent when they're red, never panicked. The UofG brand gives it institutional weight; the creative layer gives it the clarity and density of a purpose-built tool.

### Aesthetic Direction

**Theme:** Light body with dark university-blue header. Matches the existing repo analysis tool and feels natural within the UofG brand (which uses the deep navy for footers and headers).

**Display font:** Chivo (Omnibus-Type) for headings and large numbers. It's a geometric grotesk with slightly condensed proportions — gives density to dashboard headings without being cold. Editorial authority without being showy. Pairs cleanly with Noto Sans for body text. Available on Google Fonts, variable weight 100-900.

```html
<link href="https://fonts.googleapis.com/css2?family=Chivo:wght@300;400;500;700;900&display=swap" rel="stylesheet">
```

**Severity palette** (derived from brand colours where possible):
- Critical: `#D4351C` (brand error) — background tint `#FDF3F1`
- High: `#E87722` (derived amber, harmonises with brand error and highlight) — background tint `#FEF5EC`
- Medium: `#C49200` (darkened brand highlight for better contrast on white) — background tint `#FFF9E6`
- Low: `#005398` (brand dark blue — "worth noting, not alarming") — background tint `#EDF4FA`
- Healthy/info: `#558B2F` (deeper green than brand success for better text contrast) — background tint `#F1F8E9`

**Fleet health score:** A single large number (0-100) using Chivo at a heavy weight. Colour derived from the score: green when healthy, amber in the middle, red when critical. This is the number the manager looks at first.

**Server cards:** Tile grid using the UofG tile pattern as a base. Each card gets a top-edge colour band (4px, using the severity palette for the worst finding). Cards show: hostname, OS, last scanned date, finding counts as small inline badges. Click to drill down.

**Layout:** Left-aligned, single-column with a max-width container (1200px, per UofG grid). The header is full-bleed dark. Content sections have generous vertical spacing. Findings tables inside detail views use the UofG table pattern. Quick wins get a distinct callout treatment — a tinted surface with the brand highlight.

**No JavaScript frameworks.** All interactivity via vanilla JS: click-to-expand server details, smooth scroll anchors, and a simple filter/search for the server grid. Everything works without JS too — progressive enhancement.

**Anti-references:** Not a SOC/SIEM dashboard (no dark mode, no glowing charts, no real-time feel). Not a generic Bootstrap admin panel. Not a university web page (no hero images, no prospectus feel). Think: the internal reporting tool that the best-run IT department already has, that other departments are jealous of.

### Design Principles
1. **Severity drives the eye.** Red things are red. Green things are green. The visual weight of a finding should match its urgency. Everything else is quiet.
2. **Scannable before readable.** A manager should understand the fleet status in under 3 seconds from the overview. Detail is one click away, never in the way.
3. **Institutional, not institutional-looking.** Use the UofG brand as a foundation of trust, not as a visual constraint. This is a tool, not a brochure.
4. **Actionable, not decorative.** Every visual element earns its space by conveying information. No sparklines, no decorative charts, no filler.
5. **Printable.** The report should look reasonable when printed or saved as PDF. No critical information lost without colour (use icons/labels alongside colour coding).
